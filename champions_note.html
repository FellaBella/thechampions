Editor's Note

Education has entered a new era. Assignments are submitted online, lessons are streamed, and communication happens instantly through digital platforms. While this transformation has created convenience and innovation, it has also exposed inequalities among students.

The digital divide is more than just a technology issue — it is an educational and social issue. Students without reliable internet, updated devices, or digital skills are placed at a disadvantage compared to their peers. As schools become more dependent on technology, the need for equal access becomes more urgent than ever.

Author’s Insight

As a student in today’s digital world, I have seen how technology can either empower or exclude. While some classmates attend virtual tutoring sessions and access unlimited online resources, others struggle just to download assignments. This gap affects academic performance, confidence, and future opportunities.

Digital access should not determine a student’s success. Schools, communities, and governments must work together to ensure that no learner is left behind simply because they lack a stable internet connection or a working device.
